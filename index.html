meshy

This is Meshy.

Motivation:

On more than one occasion, I've found myself in need of a simple (and free and accessible) tool for performing measurements, calculations, simple transformations, and other post-processing on digital meshes; the standard use case is 3D printing, e.g., jewelry. Existing tools are predominantly desktop-based, which makes them a hassle all around, hence this project.

Features: (very much under development, will add more)

OBJ and STL imports/exports; calculation of surface area, volume, center of mass (with a position indicator widget); translations, rotations, scaling, floor, center; measurement of segment length, segment angle, circle radius and circumference, arc length, cross-sectional area; given an active measurement, scaling the model so that the measurement equals a given value. Controls with the mouse (MMB to pan).

Units:

Neither STL nor OBJ files have intrinsic units. From what I gather, software will often interpret one STL unit as 1mm; else, you'll have to specify input units.

Limitations:

Primarily tested in Chrome and Firefox. Will work in the most recent versions. Appears to work in Opera. No n-gons in OBJ files; only tris and quads supported, and imported quads are triangulated. It's wisest to have them triangulated beforehand. Only one model at a time; delete the current model to uplpoad a new one. Only actions under the "Transform" tab are undoable. Not guaranteed mobile-friendly and probably never will be. File size limits depend on your system and browser; I think it's best to stay under 50MB. If the page hangs, the file's too big. Or try a different browser. I've been able to import a solid 1.5M polys.

Bugs:
